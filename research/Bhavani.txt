FEATURE 1: LIVE MARKET DASHBOARD
----------------------------------
This is the first page you see when you open the app.

What it shows:
- NIFTY 50 live price and % change
- SENSEX live price and % change
- Top 5 stocks that went UP the most today (called Gainers)
- Top 5 stocks that went DOWN the most today (called Losers)

The "Top Gainers" section might show:
  TITAN: Rs. 3,450 (+4.2%)

WHERE DOES THIS DATA COME FROM?
Yahoo Finance. The app calls Yahoo Finance's API every time
you load the page and gets the latest prices.

WHAT IS NIFTY 50?
NIFTY 50 is an index that tracks the 50 biggest companies
in India. When NIFTY goes up, it means most big companies
are doing well. When it goes down, most are falling.

WHAT IS SENSEX?
Similar to NIFTY but tracks 30 companies on BSE.

WHAT IS MARKET BREADTH?
If 35 out of 50 stocks are up today, the breadth is 70%.
High breadth = strong market. Low breadth = weak market.

FEATURE 2: STOCK INTELLIGENCE
--------------------------------
This is where you can analyse any individual stock in detail.

The app tracks 50+ NSE stocks across 10 sectors:
Banking, IT, FMCG, Auto, Pharma, Energy, Metals,
Cement, Telecom, Real Estate.

What you can do:
- Select any stock (e.g. HDFC Bank, TCS, Reliance)
- Choose a time period (1 month, 3 months, 6 months, 1 year)
- See a candlestick chart with price history
- See technical indicators: RSI, MACD, Bollinger Bands
- Get a BUY / HOLD / SELL signal
- See fundamental data: P/E ratio, market cap, etc.

WHAT IS A CANDLESTICK CHART?
Each "candle" shows 4 prices for one day:
  - Open price (where the stock started)
  - Close price (where it ended)
  - High price (the highest it went)
  - Low price (the lowest it went)
Green candle = stock went UP that day
Red candle = stock went DOWN that day

WHAT IS RSI?
RSI stands for Relative Strength Index. It is a number
between 0 and 100.
  - Above 70 = stock is "overbought" (might fall soon)
  - Below 30 = stock is "oversold" (might rise soon)
  - Between 30-70 = normal range

EXAMPLE: If TCS has RSI = 75, it means TCS has gone up
a lot recently and might be due for a correction (fall).

WHAT IS MACD?
MACD shows the momentum of a stock — is it gaining speed
going up, or slowing down?
  - MACD line above Signal line = Bullish (good sign)
  - MACD line below Signal line = Bearish (bad sign)

WHAT IS P/E RATIO?
P/E = Price to Earnings ratio.
It tells you how expensive a stock is compared to its profits.
  - P/E of 15 = you pay Rs. 15 for every Rs. 1 of profit
  - Lower P/E = cheaper stock (possibly undervalued)
  - Higher P/E = expensive stock (possibly overvalued)

EXAMPLE: If HDFC Bank has P/E = 18 and its peer ICICI Bank
has P/E = 14, ICICI might be cheaper relative to earnings.

FEATURE 3: MUTUAL FUND CENTER
--------------------------------
A mutual fund is a pool of money collected from many investors
and invested in stocks, bonds, etc. by a professional manager.

EXAMPLE: You invest Rs. 500/month in a mutual fund.
The fund manager uses your money (along with thousands of
other investors' money) to buy stocks. If the stocks go up,
your investment grows.

This feature shows 2400+ real mutual funds from AMFI.

WHAT IS AMFI?
AMFI = Association of Mutual Funds in India.
It is the official body that regulates mutual funds in India.
Every day after 8 PM, AMFI publishes the NAV of all funds.

WHAT IS NAV?
NAV = Net Asset Value. It is the price of one unit of a
mutual fund. Like a stock price, but for mutual funds.

EXAMPLE: If SBI Bluechip Fund has NAV = Rs. 65.42 today,
and you bought it when NAV was Rs. 50, your investment
has grown by 30.84%.

What you can do in this section:
- Browse funds by category (Large Cap, Mid Cap, Small Cap,
  Index Funds, ELSS, Debt, Hybrid, Gold)
- See live NAV, 1-year return, 3-year return
- Click any fund to see its NAV history chart for 1 year
- Compare funds side by side

WHERE DOES THE DATA COME FROM?
Two sources:
1. AMFI's official NAV file (updated daily)
2. mfapi.in — a free API that gives historical NAV data

FEATURE 4: SIP GOAL PLANNER
------------------------------
SIP = Systematic Investment Plan.
It means investing a fixed amount every month automatically.

EXAMPLE: You invest Rs. 5,000 every month in a mutual fund.
After 10 years, thanks to compounding, you might have
Rs. 12-15 lakhs even though you only invested Rs. 6 lakhs.

The SIP Goal Planner helps you answer this question:
"How much should I invest every month to reach my goal?"

HOW IT WORKS — STEP BY STEP:

Step 1: Choose your goal
  Options: Child's Education, Home Down Payment, Retirement,
  Wedding, Dream Vacation, Car Purchase, Emergency Fund,
  Custom Goal

Step 2: Enter your details
  - Goal name (e.g. "Daughter's MBA Abroad")
  - Target amount in today's money (e.g. Rs. 25 lakhs)
  - How many years you have (e.g. 15 years)
  - Expected inflation rate (default 6%)
  - Any existing savings for this goal

Step 3: The app calculates
  Because of inflation, Rs. 25 lakhs today will be worth
  much more in 15 years. At 6% inflation:
  Rs. 25 lakhs today = Rs. 59.9 lakhs in 15 years

  So you actually need to save Rs. 59.9 lakhs, not Rs. 25 lakhs!
  This is the "inflation-adjusted target."

Step 4: Live AMFI data is fetched
  The app downloads the AMFI NAV file and finds real
  Direct-Growth mutual funds matching each risk profile.
  It then calls mfapi.in to get the actual 3-year CAGR
  (Compound Annual Growth Rate) for those funds.

  WHAT IS CAGR?
  CAGR is the average annual return of an investment.
  If a fund had CAGR of 12%, it means on average it grew
  12% every year for the past 3 years.

Step 5: Three options are shown
  Conservative (low risk, ~7-9% returns): Rs. X/month
  Moderate (medium risk, ~11-14% returns): Rs. Y/month
  Aggressive (high risk, ~15-20% returns): Rs. Z/month

EXAMPLE:
  Goal: Child's MBA | Target: Rs. 25 lakhs | Years: 15
  Inflation-adjusted need: Rs. 59.9 lakhs

  Conservative (8% p.a.): Rs. 18,500/month
  Moderate (12% p.a.):    Rs. 12,200/month
  Aggressive (16% p.a.):  Rs. 8,100/month

Step 6: Fund recommendations
  The app shows the top 5 real mutual funds from AMFI
  for your chosen risk profile, with their live NAV
  and actual 3-year CAGR.

Step 7: AI advice
  Groq Llama 3.3 70B (an AI model) reads all your data
  and writes a personalised financial plan for you.
  It tells you: Is your goal realistic? Which profile
  should you choose? What are the risks? Pro tips?

Step 8: Save your goal
  Your goal is saved to the database. You can track
  multiple goals (education, home, retirement) all at once.

FEATURE 5: IPO INTELLIGENCE HUB
----------------------------------
IPO = Initial Public Offering.
When a private company wants to raise money from the public,
it "goes public" by selling its shares for the first time.
This is called an IPO.

EXAMPLE: Imagine a company called "ABC Engineering" needs
Rs. 150 crores to expand its business. It decides to sell
shares to the public. It sets a price band of Rs. 166-175
per share. Investors can apply to buy shares at this price.
After the IPO closes, the shares get listed on NSE/BSE and
you can buy/sell them like any other stock.

WHY DO PEOPLE APPLY IN IPOs?
Many IPOs list at a higher price than the issue price.
EXAMPLE: If you buy shares at Rs. 175 (issue price) and
the stock lists at Rs. 230 on listing day, you made
Rs. 55 profit per share on the very first day!
This is called "listing gain."

BUT NOT ALL IPOs GIVE LISTING GAINS.
Some IPOs list below the issue price. You can lose money.
This is why analysis is important.

WHAT THIS FEATURE DOES:
The app scrapes live data from ipowatch.in for every IPO.
For each IPO it shows:
  - Status: Open (you can apply now), Upcoming, or Closed
  - Price band (e.g. Rs. 166 to Rs. 175 per share)
  - Issue size (how much money the company is raising)
  - Open and close dates
  - IPO Score from 0 to 100

HOW IS THE IPO SCORE CALCULATED?
The score considers multiple real factors:
  - Issue size (larger = more institutional confidence)
  - Category (Mainboard = stricter rules = safer)
  - Subscription times (how many times oversubscribed)
  - GMP (Grey Market Premium — explained below)
  - Financial health of the company

WHAT IS GMP (GREY MARKET PREMIUM)?
Before an IPO lists on the stock exchange, shares are
traded informally in the "grey market." The premium at
which they trade is called GMP.

EXAMPLE: If an IPO has issue price Rs. 175 and GMP is
Rs. 50, it means people are willing to pay Rs. 225 for
the share before it even lists. This suggests strong
demand and a likely good listing.

WHAT IS SUBSCRIPTION?
When an IPO opens, investors apply for shares.
If the IPO receives applications for 10x more shares
than available, it is "10x subscribed."
Higher subscription = more demand = better listing chances.

EXAMPLE: Bajaj Housing Finance IPO was subscribed 63x.
That means 63 times more applications than shares available.
It listed with a 100%+ gain on listing day.

THE DETAIL MODAL (CLICK ANY IPO CARD):
When you click on any IPO card, a detailed popup opens.
It shows:
  - All important dates (open, close, allotment, listing)
  - Lot size and minimum investment amount
  - Financial KPIs of the company:
    * ROE (Return on Equity) — how efficiently the company
      uses shareholders' money. Higher is better.
      EXAMPLE: ROE of 30% means for every Rs. 100 invested,
      the company earns Rs. 30 profit.
    * ROCE (Return on Capital Employed) — similar to ROE
    * EBITDA Margin — operating profit as % of revenue
    * PAT Margin — net profit as % of revenue
    * Debt/Equity — how much debt vs equity. Lower is safer.
    * EPS (Earnings Per Share) — profit per share
    * P/E Ratio — how expensive the stock is
  - Revenue and PAT history for 3-4 years
    (Is the company growing? Is profit increasing?)
  - Peer comparison (how does this company compare to
    similar listed companies in terms of P/E and RoNW?)
  - Promoter holding (what % do the founders own?
    Higher promoter holding = more confidence in the company)
  - Use of IPO proceeds (what will the company do with
    the money raised? Expansion? Debt repayment?)
  - IPO Quality Radar chart (visual representation of
    ROE, PAT Margin, EBITDA, Timing, Category)

THE AI EXIT STRATEGY (THE MOST IMPORTANT PART):
The Groq AI analyses ALL the above data and gives you
a complete 7-section report:

  1. VERDICT — APPLY / AVOID / NEUTRAL with reason
  2. INVESTMENT THESIS — 3 specific reasons to apply/avoid
  3. KEY RISKS — 3 honest risks to be aware of
  4. FINANCIAL HEALTH — Strong/Moderate/Weak with reasoning
  5. VALUATION CHECK — Is it priced fairly vs competitors?
  6. PROFIT MAXIMISATION STRATEGY:
     - Should you sell on listing day? At what % gain?
     - What to do after 30 days?
     - What to do after 60-90 days?
     - Recommended split strategy
       (e.g. "Sell 50% on listing day if gain > 20%,
        hold remaining 50% for 90 days for long-term gains")
     - Stop loss level (at what % loss should you exit?)
  7. WHO SHOULD APPLY — Listing gain seekers / Long-term /
     Both / Neither

EXAMPLE AI ADVICE:
"APPLY. Om Power Transmission has strong financials with
ROE of 35.83% and PAT Margin of 7.84%. Revenue has grown
from Rs. 121 Cr to Rs. 281 Cr in 3 years.

Profit Strategy: Sell 60% on listing day if gain exceeds
15%. Hold remaining 40% for 90 days targeting Rs. 220-230.
Stop loss: Exit completely if stock falls below Rs. 160
(8.5% below issue price of Rs. 175)."

FEATURE 6: SMART MONEY TRACKER
---------------------------------
"Smart money" refers to institutional investors — big banks,
mutual fund companies, foreign investors. They have research
teams and billions of rupees. When they buy, prices usually go up.
When they sell, prices usually fall.

FII = Foreign Institutional Investors
(Foreign companies/funds investing in Indian markets)

DII = Domestic Institutional Investors
(Indian mutual funds, insurance companies, banks)

EXAMPLE: If FIIs buy Rs. 3,000 crores worth of Indian stocks
today, it means foreign investors are confident in India.
This is usually a bullish (positive) signal.

If FIIs sell Rs. 2,000 crores, it means they are pulling
money out of India. This is usually a bearish (negative) signal.

WHERE DOES THIS DATA COME FROM?
Directly from NSE India's official API:
https://www.nseindia.com/api/fiidiiTradeReact
This is the same data that financial news channels show.

WHAT THIS FEATURE SHOWS:
  - FII Net Flow today (buy - sell = net)
  - DII Net Flow today
  - FII Buy amount and Sell amount
  - Market Signal: STRONG BUY / BUY / SELL / STRONG SELL / MIXED
  - Bulk Deals (large trades by institutions)
  - Block Deals (off-market large trades)
  - Sector-wise money flow (which sectors are getting money?)
  - Volume analysis (which stocks have unusual trading activity?)

EXAMPLE:
  FII Net: +Rs. 2,800 Cr (they bought more than they sold)
  DII Net: +Rs. 1,200 Cr (domestic funds also buying)
  Signal: STRONG BUY

This means both foreign and domestic institutions are
buying Indian stocks today. Good sign for the market.

FEATURE 7: PORTFOLIO & RISK MANAGER
--------------------------------------
This is your personal stock portfolio tracker.

HOW TO USE IT:
1. Go to "Add Stock" tab
2. Select a stock (e.g. HDFCBANK)
3. Enter how many shares you bought (e.g. 10 shares)
4. Enter the price you paid (e.g. Rs. 1,600 per share)
5. Enter the date you bought it
6. Select the sector (Banking)
7. Click "Add to Portfolio"

The app immediately fetches the current live price from
Yahoo Finance and shows you:
  - Current price: Rs. 1,720
  - Your investment: Rs. 16,000 (10 x 1,600)
  - Current value: Rs. 17,200 (10 x 1,720)
  - Profit: Rs. 1,200 (+7.5%)

WHAT IS TODAY'S P&L?
P&L = Profit and Loss.
Today's P&L shows how much you gained or lost TODAY only.
If HDFC Bank was Rs. 1,710 yesterday and Rs. 1,720 today,
your today's gain = Rs. 10 x 10 shares = Rs. 100.

CHARTS AVAILABLE:
  - Portfolio allocation pie chart (what % is in each stock)
  - P&L bar chart (which stocks are profitable, which are not)
  - Return % chart (sorted from best to worst performer)

RISK METRICS:
The app also calculates professional risk metrics:
  - Portfolio Beta: How much your portfolio moves vs NIFTY
    Beta = 1.0 means it moves exactly like NIFTY
    Beta = 1.5 means it moves 50% more than NIFTY (riskier)
    Beta = 0.7 means it moves less than NIFTY (safer)
  - Sharpe Ratio: Return per unit of risk (higher = better)
  - VaR (Value at Risk): Maximum expected loss on a bad day
    with 95% confidence
  - Max Drawdown: Biggest fall from peak to bottom

EXAMPLE:
  Your portfolio has Beta = 1.2 and Sharpe Ratio = 1.4
  This means: slightly riskier than market, but giving
  good returns for the risk taken.

FEATURE 10: NEWS & SENTIMENT ANALYSIS
----------------------------------------
Every day, hundreds of news articles are published about
the Indian stock market. Reading all of them is impossible.
This feature does it for you automatically.

DATA SOURCES (all live RSS feeds):
  - Google Finance (Indian market news)
  - Economic Times Markets section
  - Moneycontrol

WHAT IS SENTIMENT ANALYSIS?
Sentiment analysis is the process of determining whether
a piece of text is positive, negative, or neutral.

EXAMPLE:
  Headline: "RBI cuts interest rates, market rallies"
  Sentiment: POSITIVE (good news for stocks)

  Headline: "FIIs sell Rs. 5,000 Cr, market falls sharply"
  Sentiment: NEGATIVE (bad news for stocks)

  Headline: "SEBI announces new regulations for mutual funds"
  Sentiment: NEUTRAL (informational, not clearly good or bad)

HOW IT WORKS:
The app uses two tools to score each headline:
  1. TextBlob — analyses the language and grammar
  2. VADER — specifically designed for financial text

Each headline gets a score from -1 (very negative) to
+1 (very positive). 0 is neutral.

WHAT YOU SEE:
  - All latest headlines with their sentiment score
  - Colour coding: Green = Positive, Red = Negative
  - Sector-wise sentiment heatmap
    (Which sectors are getting positive/negative news?)
  - Sentiment timeline chart (is news getting better or worse?)
  - Distribution pie chart (how many positive vs negative?)

EXAMPLE:
  Today's news: 12 Positive, 5 Negative, 8 Neutral
  IT sector: Positive (TCS results beat expectations)
  Banking sector: Negative (RBI rate concerns)
  Overall market mood: Cautiously Positive

FEATURE 12: ADVANCED ANALYTICS
---------------------------------
This section is for more experienced investors who want
deeper market analysis.

WHAT IT INCLUDES:

SECTOR HEATMAP:
A colour-coded grid showing which sectors are up or down.
Green = sector is up today
Red = sector is down today
Darker colour = bigger move

EXAMPLE:
  IT: +2.1% (dark green)
  Banking: -1.2% (light red)
  Pharma: +0.3% (light green)
  Metals: -2.8% (dark red)

CORRELATION MATRIX:
Shows how two stocks move together.
Correlation of +1 = they always move in the same direction
Correlation of -1 = they always move in opposite directions
Correlation of 0 = no relationship

WHY IS THIS USEFUL?
If you own HDFC Bank and ICICI Bank, and their correlation
is 0.92 (very high), you are not really diversified.
If one falls, the other will likely fall too.
Better to own stocks with lower correlation for true
diversification.

VOLUME INTELLIGENCE:
Shows which stocks have unusually high trading volume today.
If a stock normally trades 1 million shares per day but
today it traded 3 million shares, that's 3x normal volume.
High volume + price increase = strong buying interest
High volume + price decrease = strong selling pressure

MARKET BREADTH GAUGE:
A speedometer-style gauge showing overall market health.
Above 60% = Bullish market
Below 40% = Bearish market
40-60% = Neutral/Mixed market

PART 7: COMMON QUESTIONS FROM NEW USERS
----------------------------------------------------------------

Q: Is this app safe to use?
A: Yes. The app only reads data from public sources.
   It does not ask for your bank details, Demat account,
   or any personal information. Your portfolio data is
   stored only on your own computer.

Q: Can I actually invest through this app?
A: No. This app is for analysis and education only.
   To actually invest, you need a Demat account with
   a broker like Zerodha, Groww, or Upstox.
   This app helps you DECIDE what to invest in.
   The actual investing happens on your broker's platform.

Q: Is the data always accurate?
A: The data is fetched live from official sources (NSE,
   AMFI, Yahoo Finance). However, there can be small
   delays of a few minutes. For critical investment
   decisions, always verify on your broker's platform.

Q: What if the app shows "No data" or "N/A"?
A: This usually means the data source is temporarily
   unavailable. Try refreshing after a few minutes.
   AMFI data is only updated once per day after 8 PM IST.

Q: Why does the first load take so long?
A: The first load fetches live data from multiple sources
   simultaneously. After that, the app caches the data
   for a few minutes so subsequent loads are fast.

Q: What is the difference between Streamlit and React?
A: Streamlit is a Python framework that creates simple
   web apps quickly. React is a JavaScript framework
   for building professional websites. Both show the
   same data. The React version looks more polished
   and is better for presentations.

Q: Do I need to know programming to use this?
A: No. Once the app is running, you just use it like
   any website. You don't need to understand the code.
   The setup (installing Python, running commands) is
   a one-time process.

Q: What is "Groq Llama 3.3 70B"?
A: Groq is a company that provides fast AI inference.
   Llama 3.3 70B is an AI language model made by Meta
   (the company that owns Facebook). "70B" means it has
   70 billion parameters — it is a very powerful model.
   Groq makes it available for free through their API.
